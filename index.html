THE PROPOSAL

DOMINIC planned for us an engagement-road-trip. Though at the time I thought it was just a regular-old road-trip. We left the Bay Area and headed to LA where we stayed with friends and went to Disneyland. We then headed down to San Diego to soak up some sun and visit the Zoo. Next we visited family in Tucson and saw an A's Spring Training game. At this point, the trip was already fun and memorable. A quick stop in Sedona to see the red rocks and we were on our way to the Grand Canyon. The first night a huge snow storm hit. It was beautiful and unexpected. The next day we walked around the canyon and soaked up the breathtaking views. We looked up the time for sunset and went back to the room. I wanted to take a nap but Dominic told me I could not because he did not want to miss the sunset. I remember arguing with him and saying that we had plenty of time, but he insisted we leave an hour before the sunset to take the tram a few short miles.

Well, when we got there we sat in half a dozen different spots, Dominic really wanted to see the sunset. Once we were perfectly comfy and the sun started to go down Dominic proposed and I immediately starting nodding and crying. I had no idea. He was shaking and I was crying and we decided that we better not drop the ring into the huge canyon before us. We got back on the tram and the other riders must have thought we were crazy-shaking and crying and with huge smiles on our faces. In hindsight I can see that he was being strange the whole day but I didn't think anything of it. He was so nervous, even though he knew I'd say yes. My perfect ring was purchased at my favorite vintage jewelry store in New York where I'd love to wander and drool over the jewelry. I will never forget my perfect proposal.

- Rachel David
